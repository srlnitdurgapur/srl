Systems Research Lab

human computer interaction

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is a subfield of computer science that focuses on how people interact with computers.
Rural HCI is a specific area within HCI that examines how people in rural areas use technology, and aims to design technology that is accessible and useful for them.

Mobile computing

Mobile Computing refers a technology that allows transmission of data, voice and video via a computer or any other wireless enabled device. It is free from having a connection with a fixed physical link. It facilitates the users to move from one physical location to another during communication.

Speech Processing

Speech Processing is a field of study that focuses on enabling machines to understand and produce human speech. It involves developing algorithms and techniques for tasks such as automatic speech recognition, speech synthesis, speaker recognition, and natural language processing.

NLP

Natural language processing (NLP) refers to the branch of computer science—and more specifically, the branch of artificial intelligence or AI—concerned with giving computers the ability to understand text and spoken words in much the same way human beings can.

5G Communication

Fifth Generation (5G) Technology in wireless communication, which is a recent development in mobile networks. It presents evaluations of mobile communication technology and how challenges in each evolution were addressed with the help of next-generation mobile networks.

IOT

IoT stands for "Internet of Things," and it refers to the network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other objects that are embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, allowing them to exchange data and interact with each other and with their environment.

Drone Technology

Drone technology involves the design and operation of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). Drones can be controlled remotely or operate autonomously, and are equipped with sensors and cameras to collect data and images from the air. They have various applications, but there are also concerns around privacy, security, and safety.

Cellular Automata

Cellular Automata is a computational model that uses a grid of cells, with each cell having a finite number of states that change over time based on a set of rules depending on its neighbors' states. It is used to simulate complex systems in physics, biology, computer science, and economics.
