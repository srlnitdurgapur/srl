Running Projects
KiOSK
Funded by: West Bengal ,DST
Name of the PI : Prof. Subrata Nandi

KiOSKrefers to the development and implementation of a self-service interactive computer terminal, typically used to provide information, sell products, or offer services. The KiOSK project can be used in various industries such as retail, healthcare, hospitality, education, and more. The operating system is divided into a set of core components that can be combined and customized as needed.Kiosk systems are often used to streamline customer service, reduce wait times, and increase efficiency.It can also include ongoing maintenance and support, including software updates and hardware repairs, to ensure the system operates efficiently and effectively over time.

CityProbe
Funded by: IMPRINT, Govt. of India
Name of the PI : Prof. Subrata Nandi
Name of the CoPI's : Dr. Sujoy Saha and Dr. Mousumi Saha

CityProbe is a research project that aims to develop a network of sensors and data analysis tools to gather and analyze data about urban environments. The project focuses on developing low-cost sensors that can be deployed throughout a city to collect real-time data on various environmental factors such as air quality, noise levels, and traffic patterns.the CityProbe project aims to leverage technology and data to improve urban environments, increase sustainability, and enhance the overall quality of life for residents in cities around the world.

Completed Projects
Post-Disaster Situation Analysis and Resource Management Using Delay-Tolerant Peer-to-Peer Wireless Networks (DISARM)
Funded by: ITRA, Govt. of India
Name of the PI : Prof. Subrata Nandi
Name of the CoPI's : Dr. Sujoy Saha

Post-Disaster Situation Analysis and Resource Management Using Delay-Tolerant Peer-to-Peer Wireless Networks (DISARM) is a research project that proposes a new approach to managing communication and resources during post-disaster situations.In a disaster situation, the DISARM system would rely on wireless nodes that can communicate with each other in a peer-to-peer fashion, without requiring a centralized network. the DISARM project represents an innovative approach to disaster management that could help improve communication and resource allocation during times of crisis.
