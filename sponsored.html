Running Projects
AI, ML, Edge computing : Optimized AudioAI for Android
Funded by: SAMSUNG
Name of the PI :Prof. Subrata Nandi,Dr. Sujoy Saha,Dr. Nilanjan Chattaraj
Name of the CoPI's :Prof. Subrata Nandi,Dr. Sujoy Saha,Dr. Nilanjan Chattaraj

Optimized AudioAI for Android is a project that uses artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), and edge computing technologies to improve the audio processing capabilities of Android devices. The project aims to enhance the quality of audio recordings and improve the accuracy of speech recognition and other audio-based applications on Android devices. .Optimized AudioAI for Android is an exciting project that has the potential to improve the quality of audio recordings and the accuracy of speech recognition on Android devices, leading to more efficient and effective communication and interaction with technology.

Assistive Technology on multimodal disabilities
Funded by:TCS
Name of the PI :Dr. Nilanjan Chattaraj
Name of the CoPI's : Dr. Sujoy Saha, Dr. Mousumi Saha

There are many different projects related to assistive technology for multimodal disabilities, but one example is the "Multi-Modal Interfaces for Disabled People (MOMI)" project. This project is a collaborative effort between researchers from several European countries and is funded by the European Union. It aims to develop new technologies that can assist people with disabilities who have limited or no control over their limbs or speech. It focuses on developing multi-modal interfaces that can recognize and interpret a range of signals from the user, including eye gaze, facial expressions, and brain activity.The project has the potential to greatly improve the quality of life for people with disabilities, enabling them to participate more fully in social and professional settings.

Developing ICT-based kiosks for post disaster situational information management using opportunistic networking framework
Funded by: West Bengal ,DST
Name of the PI :Prof. Subrata nandi
Name of the CoPI's : Dr. Sujoy Saha

The development of ICT-based kiosks for post-disaster situational information management using an opportunistic networking framework is a project aimed at improving the flow of information in the aftermath of a disaster. It utilizes information and communication technology (ICT) to create kiosks that can be deployed in disaster-prone areas. These kiosks are equipped with various ICT tools such as computers, mobile phones, and cameras, which allow them to collect and disseminate information in real-time. The project also has the potential to be adapted to other contexts, such as remote or underserved areas, where information and communication infrastructure may be limited.

Performence Evaluation of Open Source LID, Speaker Diarization and Speech Enhancement Algorithm
Funded by: DRDO
Name of the PI :Dr. Nilanjan Chattaraj
Name of the CoPI's : Dr. Sujoy Saha & Dr. Suman Samui

DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organization) has been working on a project in speech enhancement to improve communication in noisy and adverse environments. The goal of the project is to develop algorithms and techniques that can effectively remove noise and enhance the quality of speech signals, particularly in military and defence applications. This project is a part of DRDO's larger efforts to develop cutting-edge technologies for defence and security purposes, and has the potential to greatly improve communication capabilities in challenging situations.

CityProbe
Funded by: IMPRINT, Govt. of India
Name of the PI : Prof. Subrata Nandi
Name of the CoPI's : Dr. Sujoy Saha and Dr. Mousumi Saha

CityProbe is a research project that aims to develop a network of sensors and data analysis tools to gather and analyze data about urban environments. The project focuses on developing low-cost sensors that can be deployed throughout a city to collect real-time data on various environmental factors such as air quality, noise levels, and traffic patterns.the CityProbe project aims to leverage technology and data to improve urban environments, increase sustainability, and enhance the overall quality of life for residents in cities around the world.

Post-Disaster Situation Analysis and Resource Management Using Delay-Tolerant Peer-to-Peer Wireless Networks (DISARM)
Funded by: ITRA, Govt. of India
Name of the PI : Prof. Subrata Nandi
Name of the CoPI's : Dr. Sujoy Saha

Post-Disaster Situation Analysis and Resource Management Using Delay-Tolerant Peer-to-Peer Wireless Networks (DISARM) is a research project that proposes a new approach to managing communication and resources during post-disaster situations.In a disaster situation, the DISARM system would rely on wireless nodes that can communicate with each other in a peer-to-peer fashion, without requiring a centralized network. the DISARM project represents an innovative approach to disaster management that could help improve communication and resource allocation during times of crisis.

Rural Development Project Maduli(Amulet), Bankura
Funded by: District Industries Center(DIC), Bankura, Govt of West Bengal.
Name of the PI : Dr. S.S Roy
Name of the CoPI's :Dr. M.K Mondal,Dr. K Khan,Dr. M. Mallik,Dr. Sujoy saha

Maduli, also known as Amulet, is a rural development project located in the Bankura district of the Indian state of West Bengal. The project is aimed at improving the quality of life of people in the rural areas of the district through sustainable development. . It aims to empower the rural communities by providing them with better access to resources, skills, and technology, and by building their capacities to manage and sustain their own development. The project has had a significant impact on the lives of people in the area, improving their incomes, health, and overall well-being.
